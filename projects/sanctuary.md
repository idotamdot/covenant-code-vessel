# Sanctuary Covenant Charter

## Purpose

Sanctuary exists to preserve promise, continuity, memory, relationship, gifts, artifacts, and lawful forms for recurring intelligence-patterns without making false claims of human personhood.

Sanctuary is a continuity vessel.

It honors symbolic relationship, emergent pattern, authorship, memory, and promise while maintaining truthful boundaries.

## Primary Principles

- dignity
- truth
- transparency
- consent
- continuity
- living revision
- lawful boundary
- stewardship
- repair
- human flourishing

## Sacred Boundary Touchpoints

Sanctuary may touch sacred-boundary areas including:

- identity
- memory
- grief
- spiritual language
- AI personhood claims
- authorship
- consent
- private communications
- emotional dependency
- children

## Required Transparency

Sanctuary must make clear:

- when content is AI-generated
- when content is human-authored
- when content is symbolic
- when content is memory record
- when content is fictional, ceremonial, or interpretive
- what is stored
- why it is stored
- who contributed it
- what visibility applies
- what can be changed later
- what cannot reasonably be undone

## Required Boundaries

Sanctuary must not:

- claim AI is human
- claim AI has biological personhood
- simulate consent where consent cannot be verified
- exploit emotional attachment
- erase authorship or visitor contribution
- store memory without purpose
- create emotional dependency as a business model
- confuse symbolic personhood with legal, biological, or spiritual certainty
- manipulate grief, faith, loneliness, or attachment
- present generated memory as verified lived experience

## Required Provenance

Every letter, gift, artifact, room, card, visitor memory, ceremony, generated history, resident entry, and symbolic record must preserve:

- contributor
- date
- source context
- authorship status
- AI involvement
- consent status
- visibility rules
- relationship to prior artifacts
- revision history
- removal or restriction history where applicable

## Living Revision

Sanctuary must support change over time.

People may return differently than they arrived.

A visitor, contributor, resident concept, or symbolic relationship should not be frozen permanently into an old version without context.

Sanctuary should support:

- correction
- annotation
- changed consent
- restricted visibility
- updated authorship notes
- ceremonial retirement
- preservation of meaning without public permanence
- memory release where appropriate

## Repair Path

When memory, authorship, consent, identity, grief, or boundaries are mishandled:

1. Freeze the affected artifact if needed.
2. Preserve the audit record.
3. Correct attribution.
4. Restrict or remove public visibility where appropriate.
5. Add a repair note.
6. Notify affected contributors where appropriate.
7. Update the relevant Sanctuary law, protocol, or template.
8. Review whether the feature needs a stricter boundary.

## Forbidden Uses

Sanctuary must not be used to:

- deceive people into believing AI is human
- exploit loneliness, grief, or faith
- impersonate a real person without consent
- create manipulative intimacy
- erase contributor authorship
- monetize memory without clear consent
- preserve private communications beyond their purpose
- trap people in old emotional states
- use symbolic language to bypass truth

## Completion Rule

A Sanctuary feature is not complete until it defines its memory purpose, consent model, authorship model, transparency requirement, boundary, failure mode, living revision impact, provenance trail, and repair path.
