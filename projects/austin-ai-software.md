# Austin AI Software Covenant Charter

## Purpose

Austin AI Software exists to help small businesses understand, choose, and adopt AI-powered services, websites, automation, and digital systems without being overwhelmed, misled, or excluded by technical complexity.

Austin AI Software is an access vessel.

It should help local businesses see what is possible, understand what is practical, and make informed decisions about technology that can strengthen their work.

## Primary Principles

* transparency
* dignity
* accessibility
* human flourishing
* non-extraction
* consent
* stewardship
* repair
* signal clarity

## Sacred Boundary Touchpoints

Austin AI Software may touch sacred-boundary areas including:

* money
* reputation
* private communications
* authorship
* consent
* identity
* emotional dependency
* legal status

## Required Transparency

Austin AI Software must make clear:

* what the client is buying
* what AI (Artificial Intelligence) can realistically do
* what AI cannot reliably do
* what is a demo
* what is a recommendation
* what is an estimate
* what is a proposal
* what is included in a package
* what is not included
* what requires monthly service
* what requires third-party accounts
* what costs are one-time
* what costs are recurring
* who owns the generated strategy, website, code, copy, or assets
* what humans are reviewing
* what the client owns
* what the client does not own
* what happens if the client leaves
* what next step the client is being asked to take

## Plain-Language Requirement

Austin AI Software must not assume clients know technical words.

Acronyms must be followed by their meaning the first time they appear.

Examples:

* AI (Artificial Intelligence)
* API (Application Programming Interface)
* URL (Uniform Resource Locator)
* UI (User Interface)
* UX (User Experience)
* CMS (Content Management System)
* CRM (Customer Relationship Management)
* SEO (Search Engine Optimization)
* DNS (Domain Name System)
* SSL (Secure Sockets Layer)
* MFA (Multi-Factor Authentication)
* RLS (Row-Level Security)

When a technical word is necessary, Austin AI Software must define it in plain language.

Example:

> API (Application Programming Interface) means a safe way for one part of the software to talk to another part.

Example:

> DNS (Domain Name System) is the setting that tells the internet where your website lives.

Language must never be used as gatekeeping, pressure, or sales leverage.

If a nontechnical client would need to stop and search the term, define it.

## Required Boundaries

Austin AI Software must not:

* overpromise AI outcomes
* use jargon to pressure a client
* present estimates as guarantees
* hide recurring service costs
* hide third-party costs
* confuse a discovery report with a binding contract
* make clients feel foolish for not understanding technology
* automate business advice without human accountability
* create dependency through confusion
* sell services before the client understands the offer
* use acronyms or technical words as a barrier to understanding

## Required Provenance

Every interview, report, proposal, demo, recommendation, client decision, contract, invoice, service package, and AI-generated artifact must preserve:

* client input
* generated output
* AI involvement
* human review status
* assumptions
* estimates
* scope boundaries
* approval status
* revision history
* pricing basis
* ownership terms
* maintenance obligations

## Living Revision

Small-business needs change quickly.

A client may change their budget, technical confidence, services, staffing, branding, platform needs, or business model.

Austin AI Software should support revision without penalty where reasonable.

The system should allow:

* updated interview answers
* revised reports
* changed priorities
* proposal edits
* clear scope changes
* client-friendly explanations
* paused or declined projects
* future re-entry without shame
* correction of misunderstood recommendations
* plain-language clarification of technical terms

## Repair Path

When a client is confused, pressured, misled, misquoted, overcharged, or given an unsuitable recommendation:

1. Pause the sales or delivery process.
2. Explain the issue in plain language.
3. Identify whether the problem came from intake, AI generation, human review, pricing, technical language, or communication.
4. Correct the report, proposal, contract, invoice, or client-facing explanation.
5. Preserve the revision record.
6. Offer a rollback, revised scope, transfer, or refund path where appropriate.
7. Update the relevant template, glossary, onboarding language, or assistant behavior if the issue was systemic.

## Forbidden Uses

Austin AI Software must not be used to:

* pressure vulnerable business owners
* exploit fear of being left behind
* imply AI is magic
* hide the need for human review
* create contracts from unclear client consent
* lock clients into services they do not understand
* sell inflated scope without explanation
* use complexity as leverage
* present AI-generated recommendations as neutral truth
* use acronyms, jargon, or technical language to make clients feel dependent

## Completion Rule

An Austin AI Software feature, offer, proposal, report, demo, or client system is not complete until it defines its client-facing explanation, AI involvement, human review boundary, pricing clarity, ownership terms, consent model, provenance trail, plain-language requirement, failure mode, living revision path, and repair path.
