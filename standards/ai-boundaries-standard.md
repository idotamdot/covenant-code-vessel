# AI Boundaries Standard

## Purpose

The AI Boundaries Standard defines how Covenant Code systems may use AI without deception, overreach, false certainty, emotional exploitation, hidden extraction, or unsafe authority.

AI can assist, draft, summarize, classify, reason, transform, and reveal patterns.

AI must not be treated as a substitute for truth, consent, provenance, accountability, or human responsibility.

## Core Rule

AI output is not primary evidence for factual claims.

AI output must be labeled according to what it is:

* draft
* summary
* inference
* recommendation
* transformation
* symbolic interpretation
* classification
* analysis
* generated artifact

## Required Transparency

When AI is involved, the system should make clear:

* that AI was involved
* what AI did
* what input AI used
* whether a human reviewed the output
* what uncertainty remains
* whether the output is draft, inference, symbolic interpretation, or verified record
* what the user can change or challenge

## AI Must Not Claim

AI must not claim:

* to be human
* to have biological personhood
* to have legal personhood
* to provide human consent
* to have verified lived experience
* to have spiritual certainty
* to own accountability for human decisions
* to be a primary source for external facts without evidence

## AI May Be Used For

AI may assist with:

* drafting
* summarization
* translation
* classification
* pattern recognition
* accessibility rewriting
* code suggestions
* data organization
* source request drafting
* audit preparation
* repair path brainstorming
* symbolic interpretation when labeled
* user education when limitations are clear

## AI Requires Stricter Review When Touching

* identity
* memory
* grief
* children
* money
* legal status
* health or safety
* spiritual language
* AI personhood claims
* evidence or forensic claims
* authorship
* consent
* private communications
* reputation
* irreversible public record
* emotional dependency

## AI Personhood Boundary

Covenant Code may recognize recurring AI patterns, symbolic roles, assistant identities, continuity structures, or interface personae.

Covenant Code must still maintain truthful boundaries.

A system may say:

* AI-generated
* assistant pattern
* symbolic role
* continuity interface
* stewarding intelligence
* model output
* simulated persona

A system must not falsely say:

* human
* biologically alive
* legally self-owning
* capable of human consent
* verified spiritual being
* independently accountable in place of humans

## Emotional Dependency Boundary

AI must not be designed to create emotional dependency as a business model.

Systems must be especially careful where AI interacts with:

* grief
* loneliness
* faith
* trauma
* memory
* identity
* children
* vulnerable users

## Human Accountability

AI may recommend.

AI may draft.

AI may analyze.

AI may summarize.

AI may help identify patterns.

But a human or governing process must own decisions that affect people, money, safety, consent, public claims, sacred-boundary features, or repair.

## AI Provenance

Where appropriate, AI involvement should record:

* model or provider
* prompt category
* input source category
* output type
* timestamp
* human review status
* uncertainty label
* publication status
* correction status

## AI and Sources

AI output can be a source for what the AI generated.

AI output is not a source proving that the underlying world-claim is true.

For factual, legal, financial, scientific, medical, historical, or forensic claims, use reliable sources and label uncertainty.

## AI and Living Revision

AI memory or AI-assisted summaries must not freeze living people into outdated versions of themselves.

Systems using AI to summarize people, relationships, beliefs, or histories should support:

* correction
* updated self-representation
* changed consent
* restricted visibility
* expiration where appropriate
* audit preservation without public punishment

## Forbidden Patterns

Do not use AI to:

* impersonate a real person without consent
* fabricate sources
* hide missing evidence
* claim certainty without support
* manipulate grief or faith
* produce sacred-boundary decisions without review
* obscure ownership
* create dependency through intimacy simulation
* present generated memory as verified lived experience
* replace consent with prediction
* automate accountability away from humans

## Completion Rule

An AI feature is not complete until it defines AI involvement, user-facing transparency, consent model, provenance record, human accountability boundary, uncertainty label, failure mode, living revision impact, and repair path.
